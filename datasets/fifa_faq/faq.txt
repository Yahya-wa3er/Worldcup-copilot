# FIFA WORLD CUP 2026 — ADVANCED FAQ KNOWLEDGE BASE

########################################
## SECTION 1 — GENERAL OVERVIEW
########################################

Q: What is the FIFA World Cup 2026?
A:
The FIFA World Cup 2026 is the 23rd edition of the FIFA men's international football tournament. It represents a historic expansion of the competition from 32 to 48 teams, making it the largest World Cup ever organized.

It introduces:
- A new expanded group stage format
- A multi-country hosting model
- Increased total matches (104 games)
- More global participation across all confederations

This edition is part of FIFA's long-term globalization strategy aimed at increasing competitiveness and commercial reach.

---

Q: Which countries are hosting the 2026 World Cup?
A:
The tournament is jointly hosted by:
- United States 🇺🇸 (primary host, majority of matches)
- Canada 🇨🇦 (selected group and knockout matches)
- Mexico 🇲🇽 (historic stadiums and group matches)

This is the first tri-nation World Cup in FIFA history.

## SECTION 2 — TOURNAMENT FORMAT
########################################

Q: What is the format of the 2026 World Cup?
A:
The format includes:
- 48 teams divided into 12 groups of 4 teams
- Top 2 teams per group qualify automatically
- 8 best third-placed teams also qualify
- Round of 32 introduced (new stage)
- Knockout: Round of 32 → Round of 16 → Quarterfinals → Semifinals → Final

Total matches:
104 games

---

Q: Why did FIFA change the format?
A:
Reasons include:
- Increased global representation (Africa & Asia benefit strongly)
- Higher commercial revenue
- More broadcasting rights
- Political pressure from federations wanting more slots
- Growth of football outside Europe/South America

## SECTION 3 — QUALIFICATION SYSTEM
########################################

Q: How do teams qualify?
A:
Qualification is handled by continental federations:

- UEFA (Europe): 16 spots
- CAF (Africa): 9–10 spots
- AFC (Asia): 8–9 spots
- CONMEBOL (South America): 6–7 spots
- CONCACAF (North America): 6 spots (including hosts)
- OFC (Oceania): 1 guaranteed spot

---

Q: Which teams qualify automatically?
A:
- USA 🇺🇸 (host)
- Canada 🇨🇦 (host)
- Mexico 🇲🇽 (host)

A sensor-equipped football that records:
