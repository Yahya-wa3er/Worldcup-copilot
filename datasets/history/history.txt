# FIFA WORLD CUP HISTORY — FULL KNOWLEDGE BASE

########################################
## ORIGINS
########################################

The FIFA World Cup was created in 1930 by FIFA president Jules Rimet.

- First edition: Uruguay 1930
- First winner: Uruguay 🇺🇾
- Initial concept: global football competition outside Olympics

Only 13 teams participated in the first edition.

---

########################################
## ERA EVOLUTION
########################################

## 1930–1950: FOUNDATION ERA
- Limited participation
- Travel by ship required for European teams
- Uruguay and Italy dominate early tournaments

Key event:
1950 → Uruguay defeats Brazil at Maracanã ("Maracanazo")

---

## 1954–1970: GOLDEN ERA OF BRAZIL
Brazil becomes dominant:
- 1958 → Pelé emerges (17 years old)
- 1962 → Brazil retains title
- 1970 → considered greatest World Cup team ever

Tactical evolution:
- 4-2-4 formation introduced
- attacking football dominance

---

## 1974–1990: EUROPEAN BALANCE
- West Germany rises
- Italy strong defensively
- Argentina emerges (Maradona era)

Key moment:
1986 → Maradona "Hand of God" + solo goal vs England

---

## 1990–2010: MODERN COMMERCIAL ERA
- Expansion to 32 teams (1998)
- FIFA becomes global entertainment product
- TV broadcasting explosion

Key winners:
- France 1998 (home victory)
- Brazil 2002 (Ronaldo era)
- Italy 2006 (defensive mastery)
- Spain 2010 (tiki-taka revolution)

---

## 2014–2022: MODERN HIGH INTENSITY ERA
- High pressing tactics
- Data analytics integration
- VAR introduction

Key events:
- 2014: Germany 7–1 Brazil
- 2018: France wins with young squad
- 2022: Argentina wins (Messi legacy)

---

########################################
## WORLD CUP 2026 SIGNIFICANCE
########################################

The 2026 edition represents:
- largest expansion in history (32 → 48 teams)
- globalization of football
- shift of power toward North America hosting infrastructure
- increased competitiveness of mid-tier nations

It is considered the beginning of a "new football era".
